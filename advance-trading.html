Advanced Trading Account / Mechanical trading systems

Advanced Trading Accounts, also known as mechanical trading systems, algorithmic trading, automated trading, or system trading, enable traders to establish specific rules for trade entries and exits. Once programmed, these rules can be automatically executed via a computer. In fact, various platforms report that 70% to 80% or more of shares traded on U.S. stock exchanges come from automatic trading systems.

Traders and investors can convert precise entry, exit, and money management rules into automated trading systems, allowing computers to execute and monitor trades. One of the main advantages of strategy automation is that it can remove some of the emotions from trading, as trades are automatically placed once certain criteria are met.

The rules for trade entry and exit can be based on simple conditions, such as a moving average crossover, or they can be complex strategies requiring a deep understanding of the programming language specific to the user's trading platform. Alternatively, they can be based on the expertise of a qualified�programmer.

Advantages of Automated Systems

Minimizing Emotions

Automated trading systems effectively minimize emotions throughout the trading process. By keeping emotions in check, traders generally find it easier to stick to their trading plan. Since trade orders are executed automatically once the predefined trade rules have been met, traders are unable to hesitate or question the trade. In addition to assisting traders who are hesitant to "pull the trigger," automated trading can also help restrain those who tend to overtrade, buying and selling at every perceived�opportunity.

Backtesting

Backtesting applies trading rules to historical market data to assess the viability of a trading idea. When designing a system for automated trading, all rules must be absolute, leaving no room for interpretation. The computer cannot make assumptions and must be instructed precisely on what actions to take. Traders can apply these precise sets of rules and test them on historical data before engaging in live trading.
